2.1 1. What are types of power ?

the main types you know as mechanical engineer are ** electric power and mechanical power**

$$

electric: power = I : V

$$

$$

Mechanical ; power = F ; V

$$

note that for mechanical power it could be \(F ; V\) if the velocity is linear or \(T ; omega\) if the velocity is angular .

But we will talk mainly on Fluid Power in this term.

# BONUS

Fluid Power is equal to what ?

  • it’s pressure flowrate,* or \(P*Q\).
  • \(P*V\), is Flow Energy, where \(P\) is pressure and \(V\) is volume, note that dividing energy by time gives you power, \(dfrac{V}{t}=Q\).

# BONUS

What are the units of :

  1. electric power (\(I; *V\)) \(rightarrow\) ” \(M.L^2.T^{-3}\) “.
  1. mechanical power (\(F ; *V\) or \(T* ; omega\)) \(rightarrow\) ” \(M.L^2.T^{-3}\) “.
  1. fluid power (\(P*Q\)) \(rightarrow\) ” \(M.L^2.T^{-3}\) “. ?
  • all of them have the same units which is watts, or any units with the same dimentions as watts, they only differs up to the conversion factor from one unit to another with the same dimention ” \(M.L^2.T^{-3}\) “.
  • in SI mechanical horse power (english or french) = 745.7 watts (SI),
  • watt = one joule per second
  • joule = \(kg.m^2.s^-2\)

# BONUS

why fluid power system ?

  • the fluid power system cannot increase power it controls power with increase of force and decrease velocity, according to conservation of energy it transforms mechnical energy with some losses to mechanical energy with tourque or force magnification.
  • control fluid power (\(P ;and; Q\)).
  • for electric power its limited in high power.
  • for fluid power we can control high power as wel as low power.
  • for transmission of power electric is prefered.
  • we control the tourque or force via pressurized fluid.
  • pump transform mechanical to hydraulic power and valves for control
  • pressure scaler
  • \(Q\) vector so we control value and direction.
  • then reconvert fluid power to mechnical power again via actuator.

flowchart TD

    A[mech] --> | pump conv to| B[fluid]

    B[fluid] --> |actuator conv to| A[mech]

2.2 2. What are Fluid Power Systems ?


flowchart TD

    A[Mechanical Energy T*omega] --> |takes in| B[Fluid Power System p*Q]

    B[Fluid Power System P*Q] --> |spills out| C[Mechanical Energy T*omega]

car jack takes in \(F*V\) and spills out \(F*V\) , crane which takes in rotational power and spills out rotational power .

the reason why we use fluid power system is that we want to maximize or better say control \(F\) vs \(V\) or \(Tourque\) vs \(omega\) for the same power value.

say i want 1 kw power with specific tourque or rotational velocity but not both under the same power value.

its system of power transmission via displacment of fluid, this excluds hydraulic coupling systems, but we work with bascal’s principle.

hydrulic jack multipies force but not power.

we need high force high torque.

length | 13 cm | 50 cm |

the tourque is inversly proportional with omega under constant power \(T;alpha ; dfrac{1}{omega}\).

  • According to pascal, \(Power = dfrac{F}{A}\) so if we want to lift one ton with 10 \(cm^2\) cylinder it will need twice the pressure needed by 20 \(cm^2\) cylinder.

    • According to continuity equation \(Q=A*V\) so to lift this weight with the same velocity \(V\) the 10 \(cm^2\) cylinder will take half the \(Q\) the 20 \(cm^2\) cylinder will take.

    • hydraulic power \(Power = P*Q\)
